# Review Protocol: Cloud-Fog Computing Architecture for Real-Time Digital Twins

    ## 1. Background and Rationale

    Digital twins are virtual representations of physical systems that enable real-time monitoring, simulation, and optimization. The integration of cloud-fog computing architectures presents opportunities for improved performance, reduced latency, and enhanced scalability in digital twin implementations.

    ## 2. Research Questions

    ### Primary Research Question
    How can cloud-fog computing architectures be designed and implemented to support real-time digital twin applications?

    ### Secondary Research Questions
    1. What are the key architectural components required for cloud-fog digital twin systems?
    2. What performance metrics are critical for evaluating real-time digital twin systems?
    3. What are the main challenges in implementing cloud-fog architectures for digital twins?
4. What solutions and best practices have been proposed in the literature?

## 3. Search Strategy

### 3.1 Databases
- IEEE Xplore
- ACM Digital Library
- Springer Link

### 3.2 Search Terms
- **Primary terms**: "digital twin", "cloud computing", "fog computing", "edge computing"
- **Secondary terms**: "real-time", "architecture", "IoT", "Industry 4.0"
- **Combined search strings**: See `../02_search/search_strings.txt`

### 3.3 Time Period
Publications from 2018 to present (last 8 years)

## 4. Inclusion Criteria

1. Papers focusing on cloud-fog computing architectures
2. Studies related to digital twin implementations
3. Research addressing real-time performance requirements
4. Peer-reviewed journal articles and conference papers
5. Papers written in English

## 5. Exclusion Criteria

1. Papers not related to digital twins or cloud-fog computing
2. Non-peer-reviewed publications (except highly cited white papers)
3. Duplicate publications
4. Papers without full-text access
5. Studies focusing solely on theoretical aspects without architectural considerations

## 6. Quality Assessment Criteria

Each selected paper will be evaluated based on:
1. **Relevance**: Direct relevance to cloud-fog digital twin architectures (0-3 points)
2. **Methodology**: Clear and rigorous research methodology (0-3 points)
3. **Contribution**: Novel contributions to the field (0-3 points)
4. **Validation**: Empirical validation or case studies (0-3 points)

Minimum threshold: 6/12 points

## 7. Data Extraction

For each selected study, the following information will be extracted:
- Bibliographic information (authors, year, title, venue)
- Research objectives and questions
- Architectural components and design patterns
- Technologies and platforms used
- Performance metrics and evaluation results
- Key findings and contributions
- Limitations and future work

## 8. Synthesis Method

- **Narrative synthesis**: Thematic analysis of architectural patterns and design principles
- **Quantitative synthesis**: Statistical analysis of performance metrics where applicable
- **Framework development**: Consolidation of findings into a comprehensive architectural framework
